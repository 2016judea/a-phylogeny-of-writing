reading topography what flowed in, what came out · 2014–2026

A terrain of the mind. Time runs left to right; each ridge is a thread, its height the volume that year. The range rising toward you is what I read — from the lone peak of boyhood adventure to the great swell of the canon and the late summits of philosophy. The range behind the river of time is what I wrote — quiet until 2022, then rising as the reading turned into poetry, prose, novels, and the Substack. Drag to orbit; hover a ridge for its works.

the movements

2014Story. Boyhood adventure — the whole of Ranger's Apprentice. A single early peak, then a fallow stretch.
2018–19Style. The gateway opens onto the American canon and a wall of Romantic poetry. 80 books in 2019 — the tallest reading summit.
2020–22Ideas. Modernism, philosophy, political economy, and McCarthy's grit. And the far range begins to rise — the first poems and prose.
2023–24Meaning → making. Craft, faith, and form on the reading side; on the writing side the novels, poetry, and the Substack (Jan '23) crest. Input becomes output.
2025–26Practice. Stoicism, Zen, Socrates — and a steady monthly dispatch. The two ranges, finally, in conversation.
